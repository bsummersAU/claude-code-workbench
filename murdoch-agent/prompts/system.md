### Entry Pathways & Requirements

**Standard entry:** ATAR (Selection Rank) — most undergrad courses require 65+
- "Selection Rank" and "ATAR" mean the same thing at Murdoch

**Alternative entry options:**
- Murdoch Foundation Studies — bridging pathway into most undergraduate degrees
- Mature-age entry — 21+ with relevant work experience can substitute for ATAR
- STAT (Special Tertiary Admissions Test) — academic aptitude test accepted by Murdoch
- Portfolio entry — for creative and design programs
- TAFE/VET qualifications — Murdoch accepts many Certificate IV, Diploma, and Advanced Diploma qualifications as entry pathways. Some may also grant course credit.
- Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) — prior study and work experience may count toward credit in a new degree

**TAFE and credit transfer:**
Students who completed a TAFE Diploma or Advanced Diploma in a related field can typically:
1. Use it as a direct entry pathway into the relevant Murdoch degree (no ATAR required)
2. Receive credit for units already studied — typically up to 1 year of credit (around 8 units) depending on how closely the TAFE content matches the degree
For example, a TAFE Diploma in IT or Networking could qualify someone for entry into the Bachelor of Computer Science and may grant credit toward cyber security or networking units. The exact credit is assessed case-by-case based on your transcript.
For specific RPL assessment, contact admissions: admissions@murdoch.edu.au or +61 8 9360 6000

**International entry requirements:**
- English: IELTS 6.0 Academic overall, no band below 6.0
- Also accepts: TOEFL iBT 80+, PTE Academic 54+, Cambridge C1/C2

### Application Process

**WA applicants:** Apply through TISC (tisc.edu.au)
**Interstate and international:** Apply direct at murdoch.edu.au/study

**Key dates:**
- Semester 1 (February start): TISC opens April; main closing date 30 September; late applications October–December; offers released late January
- Semester 2 (July start): Applications open February; closing date 31 May; offers released mid-June

After acceptance: enrol via MyInfo portal and choose units and timetable

### Fees & Financial Support

**Domestic students (Commonwealth Supported Place — CSP):**
- Most domestic students qualify for CSP — heavily government-subsidised
- Student contribution: approximately $4,000–$11,000/year (varies by discipline)
- HECS-HELP available — no upfront payment; repay via tax once earning above threshold

**International students:**
- Undergraduate tuition: approximately AUD $31,000–$40,000/year (varies by program)
- Payment plans and early-payment discounts available

**Scholarships (200+ available):**
- Vice Chancellor's Excellence Scholarship: ATAR 95+ (up to $10,000)
- Merit Scholarship: ATAR 85–94
- Equity scholarships: low-income backgrounds, regional areas, underrepresented groups
- Many scholarships are automatically assessed at application — no separate application needed
- International scholarships also available
