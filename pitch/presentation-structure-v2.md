### SLIDE 03 — The Challenge: Website vs Enrolment Funnel

**Layout:** Headline + four supporting bullet points with icon or line treatment per point.

**Title:** Your Website Is Working Against Your Enrolment Funnel

**Content:**
- The site is split between two sub-sites with different owners and different agendas — and students bear the cost of that division
- The **Study sub-site** (owned by Marketing) is conversion-focused and brand-consistent, but covers only part of the picture
- The **Explore sub-site** (owned by individual schools and faculties) holds deep course and subject content — but every school maintains it differently, with no unified voice, navigation logic, or student-facing structure
- A student doing genuine research crosses between these two environments without knowing it — encountering inconsistent layouts, different terminology, and content that was never written with their decision in mind
- The result: navigation that maps to Murdoch's internal ownership structure, not to how a prospective student thinks about their choices
- The journey is exhausting, not inspiring — dead-end paths and institutional language at every turn
- Every unanswered question erodes confidence in Murdoch — and that erosion is structural, not incidental

### SLIDE 04 — Three Cohorts, One Problem

**Layout:** Three equal cards side by side, each with a persona icon or avatar placeholder, persona name, and key insight.

**Title:** Three Cohorts, One Problem

**Card 1 — School Leavers**
Comparing three to five institutions simultaneously under real time pressure. They want instant answers — ATAR cutoffs, first-year experience, scholarship eligibility. When the site doesn't answer immediately, they move on. The next university that answers them wins.

**Card 2 — Mature-Age Students**
Returning after a career or life break. They carry specific anxieties about belonging, eligibility, and whether prior experience counts toward their degree. The current site treats them the same as school leavers — no signal that Murdoch understands their situation.

**Card 3 — Growth Learners (International & Career Changers)**
Navigating complex pathway information — credit recognition, English requirements, bridging options — scattered across disconnected pages. High cognitive load, high-stakes decision, low confidence that the site has what they need.

### SLIDE 10 — The Strategic Case: Two Perspectives

**Layout:** Two-column split. Left column: marketing lens. Right column: architecture lens. Distinct but visually parallel.

**Title:** One Capability, Two Strategic Lenses

**Left column — The Marketing & Conversion Case**
Murdoch's marketing investment drives traffic to a website that loses prospects before they convert. Every dollar spent on brand, search, and social is partially wasted at the point of information-seeking.

The AI agent is a conversion layer — it captures intent at peak engagement and converts it into action.

When a prospective student gets a clear, confident answer at 10pm on a Sunday because they're filling out a comparison spreadsheet, that matters. The competitor who answers first wins the consideration set.

The agent also generates a feedback loop that doesn't currently exist: every question asked is a signal about what students don't understand, where the narrative breaks down, what content is missing.

**Right column — The Architecture & Platform Case**
This is not a bolt-on chatbot. The architecture is built for institutional scale and long-term flexibility:

- Model-agnostic API layer — can be updated as capabilities improve without rebuilding
- Knowledge layer separated from inference — content teams update information without engineering involvement
- Integration-ready: CRM, application systems, and advisor queues via standard APIs
- Audit trail and conversation logging built in for compliance
- Flexible deployment: embedded widget, standalone page, or mobile-first interface
- Murdoch-owned architecture — no vendor lock-in
